## Overview

Clyros Tech offers flexible engagement models designed to meet the diverse needs of enterprise clients. Whether you require a specific project delivered to completion, ongoing capacity augmentation, or long-term operational support, we structure engagements to align with your business objectives, risk tolerance, and governance requirements.

Our engagement approach emphasizes:
- **Transparency:** Clear scope, deliverables, and accountability
- **Flexibility:** Scalable structures that adapt to changing needs
- **Governance:** Defined communication, reporting, and escalation procedures
- **Risk Management:** Appropriate commercial and operational risk sharing

## Engagement Types

### 1. Fixed-Scope Projects

**Best For:**
- Well-defined initiatives with clear requirements
- Projects requiring budget certainty and fixed timelines
- Regulatory or compliance-driven deliverables
- System migrations, implementations, or modernization efforts

**How It Works:**
- Discovery phase to establish detailed requirements and scope
- Fixed deliverables, timeline, and budget
- Milestone-based delivery with acceptance criteria
- Change order process for scope modifications
- Structured governance with steering committee oversight

**Commercial Structure:**
- Fixed-price or capped time-and-materials
- Payment tied to milestone completion
- Defined acceptance criteria for deliverables

**Typical Duration:**
3–18 months depending on scope and complexity

**Client Commitment:**
- Clear requirements and decision-making authority
- Timely review and approval of deliverables
- Access to stakeholders and subject matter experts

**Clyros Tech Commitment:**
- Deliver agreed scope on time and within budget
- Proactive risk management and issue resolution
- Knowledge transfer and documentation
- Post-delivery warranty and support period

---

### 2. Dedicated Engineering Teams

**Best For:**
- Sustained product development requiring ongoing capacity
- Organizations needing specialized skills not available internally
- Long-term technology initiatives with evolving requirements
- Capacity augmentation during peak demand periods

**How It Works:**
- Team of one or more engineers embedded within your organization
- Monthly allocation with quarterly or annual commitment options
- Team scales up or down based on your needs
- Engineers collaborate directly with your internal teams
- Shared tools, processes, and communication channels

**Commercial Structure:**
- Monthly fixed fee based on team composition
- Quarterly or annual commitment with flexible scaling
- Transparent rate card based on seniority and specialization

**Typical Duration:**
6 months to multi-year engagements

**Client Commitment:**
- Provide work direction and backlog prioritization
- Include team in planning and decision-making processes
- Provide necessary access, tools, and collaboration infrastructure

**Clyros Tech Commitment:**
- Provide qualified, experienced engineers
- Ensure continuity and minimize team turnover
- Support team with technical leadership and oversight
- Handle HR, payroll, and administrative responsibilities

---

### 3. Managed Services & Long-Term Engagements

**Best For:**
- Production systems requiring 24/7 monitoring and support
- Organizations seeking to outsource non-differentiating technology operations
- Systems requiring proactive maintenance, optimization, and evolution
- Clients prioritizing uptime, performance, and continuous improvement

**How It Works:**
- Clyros Tech assumes operational responsibility for defined systems
- SLA-based service delivery with performance metrics
- Proactive monitoring, incident management, and problem resolution
- Regular health checks, capacity planning, and optimization
- Continuous improvement and technical evolution

**Commercial Structure:**
- Monthly fixed fee or consumption-based pricing
- SLA-based pricing with performance incentives
- Tiered support levels (standard, priority, critical)

**Typical Duration:**
12 months to multi-year engagements with annual renewals

**Client Commitment:**
- Define SLA requirements and success metrics
- Provide access to systems and documentation
- Participate in regular service reviews
- Maintain escalation and approval processes

**Clyros Tech Commitment:**
- Meet agreed SLAs for availability, response time, and resolution time
- Proactive monitoring and issue prevention
- Regular reporting and service review meetings
- Continuous improvement initiatives

---

### 4. Hybrid Engagements

**Best For:**
- Complex initiatives requiring multiple engagement types
- Organizations with both project and ongoing operational needs
- Phased approaches combining delivery and support

**How It Works:**
Custom engagement structures combining elements of fixed-scope projects, dedicated teams, and managed services. For example:
- Phase 1: Fixed-scope implementation project
- Phase 2: Dedicated team for feature enhancement
- Phase 3: Managed services for ongoing operations

**Commercial Structure:**
Tailored to specific client needs with appropriate risk sharing and governance for each phase.

## Typical Timelines

### Discovery & Onboarding (2–4 weeks)
- Requirements gathering and stakeholder interviews
- Current-state assessment and documentation review
- Team introductions and logistics setup
- Access provisioning and tool configuration
- Kickoff meeting and initial planning

### Delivery Phase (variable based on engagement type)
- Fixed-scope projects: 3–18 months
- Dedicated teams: ongoing, reviewed quarterly
- Managed services: ongoing, reviewed monthly

### Knowledge Transfer & Transition (2–4 weeks)
- Documentation and runbook delivery
- Training sessions and shadowing
- Transition to client team or managed services
- Post-delivery support and stabilization

## Governance & Communication Cadence

### Daily Operations
- Daily standups or scrums (engineering team)
- Real-time communication via Slack, Teams, or email
- Issue tracking via Jira, Azure DevOps, or agreed tool

### Weekly Reporting
- Status reports covering progress, risks, and blockers
- Weekly team sync meetings
- Sprint reviews and planning (for Agile engagements)

- Executive summary and progress against milestones
- KPI and SLA reporting (for managed services)
- Resource utilization and capacity planning

### Quarterly Business Reviews
- Strategic alignment and roadmap review
- Performance assessment against success metrics
- Lessons learned and continuous improvement

Defined escalation paths for:
- Technical issues and blockers
- Scope, schedule, or budget concerns
- Quality or performance issues
- Resource or staffing changes

1. Engineering Manager / Technical Lead
2. Delivery Director / Program Manager
