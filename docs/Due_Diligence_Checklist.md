**Document Classification:** Internal Only – Confidential

**Last Updated:** January 2026

## Purpose of This Checklist

This document outlines the materials, documentation, and information that would be requested during investor due diligence, board formation, or strategic transaction. It serves as:

1. **Preparedness Assessment:** Identify gaps in documentation before diligence begins
2. **Response Planning:** Organize materials for efficient diligence response
3. **Continuous Improvement:** Maintain diligence-ready state as company matures

**This is NOT an exhaustive legal or financial diligence checklist.** Professional advisors (legal counsel, accountants, investment bankers) should be engaged for actual diligence processes.

## 10. Due Diligence Readiness Assessment

### Readiness Levels

**Level 1 – Foundational:**
- Core legal documents (incorporation, cap table)
- Active client contracts and financials
- Basic security and data protection practices
- Employee agreements and IP assignments

**Level 2 – Intermediate:**
- Organized legal, financial, and operational documentation
- Quality metrics and delivery process documentation
- HR policies and compliance framework
- Security program and incident response plan

**Level 3 – Advanced:**
- Comprehensive documentation across all categories
- Regular board reporting and governance
- External audits (financial, security, compliance)
- Mature metrics, forecasting, and strategic planning

**Current Readiness Assessment:**  
(Self-assess current level; identify gaps to address before diligence)

### Gap Closure Priorities

**Critical (Must Have Before Diligence):**
- Clean cap table and shareholder documentation
- All client contracts organized and accessible
- Financial statements accurate and complete
- IP assignments from all employees/contractors
- No outstanding legal or regulatory issues

**Important (Should Have Before Diligence):**
- Organized vendor contracts and payment records
- HR policies and employee handbook documented
- Security policies and practices documented
- Quality metrics and delivery process documentation
- Risk assessment and mitigation plans

**Nice to Have (Strengthen Diligence Response):**
- Client references and testimonials ready
- Market analysis and competitive positioning
- Strategic plan and board materials
- External audit reports (financial, security)
- Comprehensive metrics dashboard

## 11. Document Organization & Access

### Data Room Structure (Recommended)

If physical or virtual data room is established for diligence:

1. **Legal & Corporate**
2. **Contracts & Clients**
3. **Financial & Accounting**
4. **Human Resources & Team**
5. **Technology & Product**
6. **Security & Compliance**
7. **Operations & Delivery**
8. **Sales & Marketing**
9. **Strategic & Risk Management**

### Access Controls

- **Confidential Information:** NDA required before access
- **Sensitive Financial Data:** Limited to authorized parties
- **Client Information:** Anonymized or client permission required
- **Audit Trail:** Track who accesses what documents and when

### Document Retention

- **Active Engagements:** Retain all documentation
- **Completed Engagements:** Retain per contractual requirements (typically 3-7 years)
- **Financial Records:** Retain per tax law (typically 7 years)
- **Legal Documents:** Retain indefinitely
