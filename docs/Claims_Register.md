# Clyros Tech Private Limited
## Approved & Prohibited Claims Register

**Document Classification:** Internal Use Only  
**Audience:** All personnel creating external-facing content  
**Purpose:** Define specific claims that ARE and ARE NOT permitted in company communications  
**Last Updated:** January 2026

---

## PURPOSE OF THIS REGISTER

This register provides explicit guidance on what Clyros Tech MAY and MAY NOT claim in external communications.

**When in Doubt:**
- If a claim is not in the "Approved" section, assume it is NOT approved
- Escalate to Executive Leadership for evaluation
- Err on the side of NOT making the claim

**This Register is Legally Binding:**
Claims made in violation of this register may expose the company to:
- Regulatory scrutiny
- Client legal action
- Reputational damage
- Loss of business

Compliance is mandatory.

---

## HOW TO USE THIS REGISTER

### Before Making ANY Claim:

1. **Check Approved Claims:** Is this claim or a similar claim explicitly approved?
2. **Check Prohibited Claims:** Is this claim or a similar claim explicitly prohibited?
3. **Apply Common Sense Test:** Can this claim be proven with evidence?
4. **Escalate if Unsure:** Do NOT guess. Ask Executive Leadership.

### If You Need to Make a New Claim:

1. Email Executive Leadership with:
   - Proposed claim language
   - Evidence supporting the claim
   - Where/how claim will be used
   - Risk assessment
2. Wait for explicit approval (do NOT assume approval)
3. Once approved, claim will be added to this register

## SECTION B: PROHIBITED CLAIMS

These claims MAY NOT be used in any external communication under any circumstances.

### B.1 FALSE CERTIFICATIONS

| Prohibited Claim | Why Prohibited | Alternative (if needed) |
|------------------|----------------|-------------------------|
| "ISO 27001 certified" | We are not certified | "Delivery aligned with ISO-grade practices" |
| "SOC 2 compliant" or "SOC 2 certified" | We are not certified | "SOC 2–aligned security and controls" |
| "HIPAA compliant" or "HIPAA certified" | No such thing as "HIPAA certification" | "HIPAA-aware engineering for healthcare contexts" |
| "GDPR compliant" | Cannot self-certify | "Practices designed to support GDPR compliance" |
| "PCI-DSS certified" | We are not certified | "Experience with PCI-DSS requirements" |

### B.2 UNVERIFIABLE CLIENT CLAIMS

| Prohibited Claim | Why Prohibited | Alternative (if needed) |
|------------------|----------------|-------------------------|
| "Trusted by Fortune 500 companies" | Cannot verify or name clients | "Experience with enterprise clients in [industry]" |
| "Serving [specific number] clients" | Number may change; hard to verify | "Serving clients across [industries/geographies]" |
| "Client satisfaction rate of X%" | No formal survey or measurement | Omit claim or conduct actual survey |
| "[Specific company name] is our client" | Requires explicit written permission | Use only with signed client reference agreement |

### B.3 SUPERLATIVES & COMPARATIVES

| Prohibited Claim | Why Prohibited | Alternative (if needed) |
|------------------|----------------|-------------------------|
| "Best-in-class services" | Unprovable and vague | "Enterprise-grade services" |
| "Leading provider" | Unprovable | "Provider of [specific service]" |
| "Top-tier engineering talent" | Subjective and unprovable | "Senior engineers with production experience" |
| "Fastest delivery" | Unprovable and creates liability | "Efficient delivery through structured methodology" |
| "Most reliable" | Unprovable | "Focus on reliability and operational accountability" |
| "Industry-leading" | Unprovable | Simply describe service without comparative |

### B.4 SCALE & PRESENCE EXAGGERATIONS

| Prohibited Claim | Why Prohibited | Alternative (if needed) |
|------------------|----------------|-------------------------|
| "Global presence" | Implies offices we don't have | "Serving clients internationally" |
| "Offices in [specific location]" | We don't have physical offices | "Serving clients in [location] through distributed teams" |
| "Team of [inflated number] engineers" | Misrepresents scale | Use actual number or omit claim |
| "Operating in [large number] countries" | Misleading if occasional/rare | "Experience with clients in [specific regions]" |

### B.5 OUTCOME GUARANTEES

| Prohibited Claim | Why Prohibited | Alternative (if needed) |
|------------------|----------------|-------------------------|
| "Guaranteed ROI" | Creates legal liability | "Focused on measurable outcomes" |
| "Zero downtime" or "100% uptime" | Impossible to guarantee | "High-availability architecture design" |
| "Guaranteed on-time delivery" | Creates contract risk | "Structured delivery with clear milestones" |
| "Risk-free" | Nothing is truly risk-free | "Risk mitigation through [specific practices]" |
| "Problem solved" or "Issue resolved" | Oversimplifies complexity | "Engineering solution for [specific challenge]" |

### B.6 BUZZWORDS & HYPE LANGUAGE

| Prohibited Claim | Why Prohibited | Alternative (if needed) |
|------------------|----------------|-------------------------|
| "Disruptive," "revolutionary," "transformative" | Marketing hype | Describe actual service without hyperbole |
| "Cutting-edge," "bleeding-edge," "next-generation" | Vague and trendy | Name specific technologies if relevant |
| "World-class," "award-winning," "renowned" | Unprovable superlatives | Simply describe service factually |
| "Innovative solutions" | Overused and meaningless | Describe specific service or approach |
| "Digital transformation" (as primary offering) | Too vague | Specific services like "cloud migration" or "modernization" |
| "Thought leader," "visionary," "pioneer" | Self-aggrandizing | Simply provide substantive content |

### B.7 STARTUP/CULTURE LANGUAGE

| Prohibited Claim | Why Prohibited | Alternative (if needed) |
|------------------|----------------|-------------------------|
| "Rockstar engineers," "ninja developers" | Unprofessional | "Senior engineers with production experience" |
| "Amazing culture," "awesome team" | Subjective and casual | Describe actual work environment factually |
| "Fast-paced," "dynamic," "agile" (as culture) | Overused startup clichés | Omit or describe actual practices |
| "Unlimited PTO," "fun office" | Not applicable (distributed, no perks focus) | State actual benefits factually |
| "We're like a family" | Inappropriate for professional services | "Professional team with shared values" |

### B.8 ASPIRATIONAL & EMOTIONAL CLAIMS

| Prohibited Claim | Why Prohibited | Alternative (if needed) |
|------------------|----------------|-------------------------|
| "Passionate about technology" | Emotional appeal | Focus on technical capability |
| "We love what we do" | Unprofessional | Omit entirely |
| "Imagine a future where..." | Aspirational, not factual | State actual capability |
| "Your success is our success" | Cliché and vague | "Accountability for delivery outcomes" |
| "Partner in your journey" | Overused and vague | "Engineering partner for [specific service]" |

### B.9 TECHNICAL OVERCLAIMS

| Prohibited Claim | Why Prohibited | Alternative (if needed) |
|------------------|----------------|-------------------------|
| "Experts in every technology" | Impossible | List specific technologies we support |

## SECTION C: CLAIMS REQUIRING PRE-APPROVAL

These claims are NOT automatically prohibited but require Executive Leadership approval BEFORE use.

### C.1 New Service Offerings

Any claim about a service NOT explicitly listed in approved services requires:
- Documentation of capability (team skill, past delivery, etc.)
- Risk assessment (can we actually deliver this?)
- Executive Leadership sign-off

### C.2 Partnership or Alliance Claims

Claims about partnerships require:
- Signed partnership agreement
- Explicit permission from partner to mention publicly
- Executive Leadership review of language

Examples:
- "Technology partner with [vendor]"
- "Authorized reseller of [product]"
- "Joint delivery with [firm]"

### C.3 Case Study Outcomes

Specific outcome claims in case studies require:
- Client written permission
- Verifiable data (not estimates)
- Executive Leadership approval

Examples:
- "40% cost reduction"
- "50% faster deployment"
- "Zero security incidents"

### C.4 New Geographic Markets

Claims about serving NEW geographic markets require:
- Actual delivery in that market (not just inquiry)
- Understanding of local legal/regulatory requirements
- Executive Leadership approval

### C.5 Regulatory or Legal Statements

Any NEW statement about regulatory compliance, legal jurisdiction, or data protection requires:
- Legal counsel review
- Executive Leadership approval
- Addition to this register if approved

## SECTION E: CLAIM VALIDATION PROCESS

### E.1 Before Using ANY Claim

**Step 1: Check Register**
- Is claim in Section A (Approved)? → May use
- Is claim in Section B (Prohibited)? → May NOT use
- Is claim in Section C (Requires Approval)? → Escalate
- Claim not listed? → Assume NOT approved, escalate

**Step 2: Verify Evidence**
- Can this claim be proven with documentation?
- Is supporting data current and accurate?
- Would we be comfortable defending this claim under scrutiny?

**Step 3: Context Check**
- Is this claim appropriate for the audience and channel?
- Does it align with our conservative, professional tone?
- Would Executive Leadership approve this claim?

### E.2 How to Request New Claim Approval

**Email Executive Leadership with:**
1. **Proposed Claim:** Exact language you want to use
2. **Context:** Where/how claim will be used (website, proposal, deck)
3. **Evidence:** Supporting data, past deliveries, team credentials
4. **Risk Assessment:** What could go wrong if claim is challenged?
5. **Alternatives:** What would you say if this claim is denied?

**Decision Timeline:**
- Standard requests: 48-72 hours
- Urgent requests (e.g., active proposal): Escalate via phone/Slack

**Possible Outcomes:**
- **Approved:** Claim added to Section A of this register
- **Approved with Modifications:** Use revised language only
- **Conditional Approval:** Approved for specific context only
- **Denied:** Use alternative or omit claim

## SECTION F: ENFORCEMENT & VIOLATIONS

### F.1 Violation Categories

**Category 1: Minor Violation**
- Using approved claim with slight wording variation
- Failing to check register before making new claim (caught during review)
- Honest mistake with no public impact

**Category 2: Major Violation**
- Using prohibited claim (Section B) in external content
- Making unverified claims about clients or outcomes
- Publishing content with unapproved claims without review

**Category 3: Severe Violation**
- False certification claims (ISO, SOC 2, HIPAA)
- Fraudulent client or outcome claims
- Knowingly ignoring prohibition and publishing anyway

### F.2 Consequences

**Category 1:**
- Content returned for revision
- Coaching on claim validation process
- No formal disciplinary action (unless repeated)

**Category 2:**
- Content retraction if already published
- Formal written warning
- Mandatory re-training on claims governance
- Temporary loss of content creation privileges

**Category 3:**
- Immediate content retraction and public correction
- Formal disciplinary action up to and including termination
- Potential legal or financial consequences

### F.3 Reporting Violations

**If You Discover a Violation:**
1. Do NOT publish or share content further
2. Notify Executive Leadership immediately
3. Document where/when violation occurred
4. Assist with correction or retraction if needed

**Whistleblower Protection:**
Employees who report violations in good faith will NOT face retaliation, even if they were partially responsible for the violation.
