## 7. Integration Considerations

### Acquirer Integration Scenarios

**Scenario A: Full Integration (Absorption)**
- Clyros Tech brand retired; absorbed into acquirer's brand
- Team integrated into acquirer's delivery organization
- Client contracts transitioned to acquirer's paper
- **Risk:** Talent attrition; client attrition; cultural misalignment

**Scenario B: Partial Integration (Subsidiary)**
- Clyros Tech operates as subsidiary or business unit
- Brand may be preserved or co-branded
- Operational autonomy with acquirer oversight
- **Risk:** Governance complexity; cultural drift over time

**Scenario C: Standalone (Portfolio Company)**
- Clyros Tech operates independently post-acquisition
- Acquirer provides capital, strategic guidance, back-office support
- Minimal operational integration
- **Risk:** Limited synergies; may not justify acquisition premium

### Integration Risks

**1. Talent Attrition:**
- Senior engineers leave due to cultural misalignment or uncertainty
- Founder departure (if not retained) triggers team attrition

**2. Client Attrition:**
- Clients concerned about continuity or quality under new ownership
- Change of control provisions trigger contract renegotiation or termination

**3. Cultural Misalignment:**
- Acquirer's volume-focused culture conflicts with quality positioning
- Process and governance changes disrupt delivery effectiveness

**4. Operational Disruption:**
- Integration activities distract from client delivery
- System and process changes create confusion and inefficiency

### Integration Success Factors

**1. Cultural Alignment:**
- Acquirer respects and preserves quality positioning
- Engineering culture is valued and protected

**2. Talent Retention:**
- Competitive retention packages for key persons
- Transparent communication and career development opportunities

**3. Client Communication:**
- Proactive communication to clients about transaction and continuity
- Delivery continuity commitments and relationship preservation

**4. Gradual Integration:**
- Phased integration approach minimizes disruption
- Operational autonomy preserved during transition period
