# Clyros Tech Private Limited
## Leadership Transition Playbooks

**Document Classification:** Internal Only – Board Governance  
**Audience:** Board of Directors, Executive Leadership  
**Purpose:** Define transition processes for planned and emergency leadership changes  
**Last Updated:** January 2026

## Document Purpose

This document defines playbooks for leadership transitions at Clyros Tech. It provides:
- Processes for planned transitions (notice-based)
- Processes for unplanned or emergency transitions
- Communication principles (internal and client-facing)
- Stabilization period expectations
- Governance oversight during transition

**This document is NOT:**
- An indication of imminent transitions or departures
- A substitute for judgment and situational awareness
- External-facing or client-visible
- A guarantee that all scenarios are covered (adapt to situation)

## Transition Types

### Planned Transition

**Definition:**  
Leadership change with advance notice, allowing orderly transition and knowledge transfer.

**Typical Scenarios:**
- Retirement (planned departure with 3-12 months notice)
- Career change (departure for new opportunity with 30-90 days notice)
- Promotion or role change (internal transition with 30-60 days notice)
- Planned succession (founder to successor with 6-12 months transition)

**Characteristics:**
- Advance notice (30 days to 12 months)
- Incumbent cooperation (knowledge transfer, client introductions)
- Time for successor search and selection
- Overlap period (successor shadows incumbent)

---

### Unplanned Transition

**Definition:**  
Leadership change with little or no notice, requiring rapid response and interim coverage.

**Typical Scenarios:**
- Sudden resignation (departure with < 30 days notice or immediate)
- Termination (involuntary departure, immediate or short notice)
- Personal emergency (family emergency, health issue requiring leave)
- Conflict or dispute (irreconcilable differences requiring immediate separation)

**Characteristics:**
- Little or no notice (0-30 days)
- Limited or no incumbent cooperation
- Immediate interim coverage required
- Accelerated successor search

---

### Emergency Transition

**Definition:**  
Leadership change due to incapacity, requiring immediate response and emergency protocols.

**Typical Scenarios:**
- Sudden incapacity (accident, illness, hospitalization)
- Death (sudden or unexpected)
- Legal or regulatory action (arrest, investigation, disqualification)
- Force majeure (disaster, emergency, unavailability)

**Characteristics:**
- No notice (immediate)
- No incumbent cooperation (unavailable)
- Emergency authority activation
- Sensitive communication required

## Playbook 1: Planned Transition (Retirement or Career Change)

### Timeline: 3-12 Months Notice

---

### Phase 1: Notification & Planning (First 30 Days)

**Actions:**

**1. Incumbent Provides Notice**
- Incumbent notifies Executive Leadership or Board (in writing)
- Specifies departure date and transition timeline
- Confirms cooperation with transition (knowledge transfer, client introductions)

**2. Board and Executive Leadership Assess**
- Review succession plan for role
- Determine successor approach (internal promotion, external hire, interim coverage)
- Assess transition risks (client relationships, operational continuity, team morale)

**3. Develop Transition Plan**
- Define transition timeline and milestones
- Identify successor (internal or external)
- Define knowledge transfer requirements
- Develop communication plan (internal and external)
- Assign transition coordinator (typically Executive Leadership or Board member)

**4. Negotiate Transition Terms**
- Finalize departure date and transition timeline
- Negotiate transition agreement (if applicable): compensation, benefits, non-compete, confidentiality
- Define incumbent's role during transition (full-time, part-time, advisory)

**Documentation:**
- Written notice from incumbent
- Transition plan and timeline
- Transition agreement (if applicable)

---

### Phase 2: Successor Selection (30-90 Days)

**Actions:**

**1. Internal Candidate Assessment (If Applicable)**
- Assess internal candidates against role requirements
- Interview and evaluate readiness
- Make selection decision (promote or external search)

**2. External Candidate Search (If Needed)**
- Engage search firm or recruiter (if appropriate)
- Define role requirements and candidate profile
- Source and screen candidates
- Interview and evaluate finalists
- Make selection decision and extend offer

**3. Successor Onboarding Preparation**
- Prepare onboarding plan and materials
- Schedule knowledge transfer sessions with incumbent
- Arrange client introductions
- Prepare team communication

**Timeline:**
- Internal promotion: 30-60 days
- External hire: 60-120 days (depending on market and urgency)

**Documentation:**
- Candidate assessment and selection rationale
- Offer letter and employment agreement
- Onboarding plan

---

### Phase 3: Knowledge Transfer & Overlap (30-90 Days)

**Actions:**

**1. Successor Onboarding**
- Successor starts (ideally 30-60 days before incumbent departure)
- Successor shadows incumbent (observe and learn)
- Successor attends key meetings and client interactions
- Successor reviews documentation and systems

**2. Knowledge Transfer**
- Incumbent transfers critical knowledge (clients, processes, technical, relationships)
- Incumbent introduces successor to key clients and stakeholders
- Incumbent documents undocumented knowledge
- Successor asks questions and clarifies

**3. Gradual Handoff**
- Successor assumes increasing responsibility (week by week)
- Incumbent transitions from primary to advisory role
- Successor becomes primary contact for clients and team
- Incumbent remains available for questions and support

**4. Communication**
- Internal communication: Announce transition to team (timing and approach)
- Client communication: Introduce successor to key clients (calls, meetings, emails)
- External communication: Update website, LinkedIn, etc. (if appropriate)

**Timeline:**
- Overlap period: 30-90 days (depending on role complexity and successor readiness)
- Knowledge transfer: Ongoing throughout overlap period

**Documentation:**
- Knowledge transfer checklist (completed items)
- Client introduction log (who was introduced, when)
- Transition status updates (weekly or bi-weekly)

---

### Phase 4: Transition Completion (Final 30 Days)

**Actions:**

**1. Final Handoff**
- Successor assumes full responsibility
- Incumbent transitions out (final day)
- Incumbent provides final knowledge transfer and documentation
- Successor confirms readiness and understanding

**2. Post-Departure Support**
- Incumbent remains available for questions (limited period, typically 30-60 days)
- Incumbent available by phone or email (not on-site)
- Successor escalates questions or issues to incumbent as needed

**3. Stabilization**
- Successor establishes rhythm and approach
- Team and clients adjust to new leadership
- Monitor for issues or concerns (client satisfaction, team morale, operational continuity)

**4. Post-Transition Review**
- Conduct post-transition review (30-60 days after incumbent departure)
- Assess transition effectiveness (what went well, what could be improved)
- Document lessons learned
- Update transition playbook

**Documentation:**
- Final knowledge transfer sign-off
- Post-transition review report
- Lessons learned

---

### Communication Approach (Planned Transition)

**Internal Communication:**

**Timing:**  
Announce transition to team after successor is selected (not immediately upon notice)

**Message:**
- Incumbent is departing (reason: retirement, career change, etc.)
- Successor has been selected (name, background, start date)
- Transition timeline (overlap period, incumbent departure date)
- Commitment to continuity and support
- Q&A and open dialogue

**Tone:**
- Positive and supportive (celebrate incumbent's contributions)
- Confident and reassuring (successor is capable, transition is planned)
- Transparent (answer questions honestly)

---

**Client Communication:**

**Timing:**  
Introduce successor to key clients during overlap period (30-60 days before incumbent departure)

**Approach:**
- Executive-to-executive call or meeting (incumbent and successor together)
- Introduce successor (background, experience, commitment to relationship)
- Reassure continuity (incumbent is transitioning, successor is prepared)
- Establish communication cadence (regular check-ins during transition)

**Message:**
- Incumbent is transitioning (reason: retirement, career change, etc.)
- Successor has been selected (name, background, experience)
- Commitment to continuity and relationship preservation
- Successor will be primary contact going forward
- Incumbent remains available during transition (limited period)

**Tone:**
- Confident and reassuring (transition is planned, successor is capable)
- Appreciative (thank client for partnership)
- Forward-looking (excited about future with successor)

## Playbook 2: Unplanned Transition (Sudden Resignation or Termination)

### Timeline: 0-30 Days Notice

---

### Phase 1: Immediate Response (First 24-48 Hours)

**Actions:**

**1. Assess Situation**
- Determine reason for departure (resignation, termination, personal emergency)
- Assess notice period (immediate, 2 weeks, 30 days)
- Assess incumbent cooperation (willing to help, unwilling, unavailable)
- Assess transition risks (client relationships, operational continuity, team morale)

**2. Activate Interim Coverage**
- Assign interim coverage (pre-designated in succession plan)
- Notify interim coverage of assignment
- Brief interim coverage on situation and expectations
- Activate authority delegation (interim has decision-making authority)

**3. Secure Critical Information**
- Access incumbent's files, systems, and documentation
- Retrieve client lists, contact information, engagement documentation
- Secure credentials and access (email, systems, accounts)
- Preserve evidence (if termination or dispute)

**4. Notify Board and Legal Counsel**
- Notify Board of Directors (if Executive Leadership departure)
- Notify legal counsel (if termination, dispute, or legal risk)
- Assess legal and contractual implications (employment agreement, non-compete, severance)

**Documentation:**
- Departure notification (resignation letter or termination documentation)
- Interim coverage assignment
- Critical information access log

---

### Phase 2: Stabilization (First 7-14 Days)

**Actions:**

**1. Internal Communication**
- Announce departure to team (within 24-48 hours)
- Introduce interim coverage (name, role, authority)
- Reassure team (operations continue, support available)
- Answer questions and address concerns

**2. Client Communication**
- Notify key clients (within 24-48 hours)
- Introduce interim coverage (name, background, commitment to relationship)
- Reassure continuity (operations continue, quality maintained)
- Establish communication cadence (regular check-ins)

**3. Accelerated Knowledge Transfer**
- If incumbent is cooperative and available: Conduct accelerated knowledge transfer (1-2 weeks)
- If incumbent is not cooperative or unavailable: Reconstruct knowledge from documentation, team, and clients
- Document critical knowledge gaps and address urgently

**4. Assess Successor Options**
- Review succession plan (internal candidates, external pipeline)
- Determine successor approach (internal promotion, external hire, interim becomes permanent)
- Initiate successor search (if external hire needed)

**Documentation:**
- Internal communication (announcement to team)
- Client communication (notifications and reassurances)
- Knowledge transfer log (what was transferred, gaps identified)

---

### Phase 3: Successor Selection (14-90 Days)

**Actions:**

**1. Internal Candidate Assessment (If Applicable)**
- Assess interim coverage performance (can interim become permanent?)
- Assess other internal candidates
- Make selection decision (promote or external search)

**2. External Candidate Search (If Needed)**
- Engage search firm or recruiter
- Accelerated search process (target 60-90 days)
- Interview and evaluate finalists
- Make selection decision and extend offer

**3. Successor Onboarding**
- Successor starts (as soon as possible)
- Accelerated onboarding (no overlap with incumbent)
- Knowledge transfer from interim coverage and team
- Client introductions and relationship building

**Timeline:**
- Internal promotion: 14-30 days
- External hire: 60-120 days (compressed timeline)

**Documentation:**
- Candidate assessment and selection rationale
- Offer letter and employment agreement
- Onboarding plan

---

### Phase 4: Transition to Permanent Leadership (30-90 Days)

**Actions:**

**1. Permanent Successor Assumes Role**
- Successor assumes full responsibility
- Interim coverage transitions out or returns to original role
- Successor establishes rhythm and approach

**2. Stabilization**
- Monitor client satisfaction and relationships
- Monitor team morale and stability
- Address any issues or concerns promptly

**3. Post-Transition Review**
- Conduct post-transition review (60-90 days after successor starts)
- Assess transition effectiveness
- Document lessons learned
- Update transition playbook

**Documentation:**
- Transition completion confirmation
- Post-transition review report
- Lessons learned

---

### Communication Approach (Unplanned Transition)

**Internal Communication:**

**Timing:**  
Announce departure within 24-48 hours (do not delay)

**Message:**
- [Name] has departed (reason: brief, neutral, no details)
- [Interim coverage name] will assume role (background, authority, support)
- Operations continue without disruption
- Permanent successor will be identified (timeline if known)
- Q&A and support available

**Tone:**
- Calm and reassuring (operations continue, support available)
- Neutral (no blame, no drama)
- Forward-looking (focused on continuity and future)

**What NOT to Say:**
- Details of departure reason (confidential, legal risk)
- Blame or criticism of departed individual
- Speculation about future or permanent successor

---

**Client Communication:**

**Timing:**  
Notify key clients within 24-48 hours (before they hear from other sources)

**Approach:**
- Executive-to-executive call (Executive Leadership to client executive)
- Follow up with written communication (email)

**Message:**
- [Name] is no longer with company (brief, neutral, no details)
- [Interim coverage name] will assume role (background, experience, commitment)
- Operations continue without disruption
- Permanent successor will be identified (timeline if known)
- Commitment to relationship and quality

**Tone:**
- Confident and reassuring (operations continue, quality maintained)
- Appreciative (thank client for partnership)
- Forward-looking (committed to relationship)

**What NOT to Say:**
- Details of departure reason (confidential, legal risk)
- Blame or criticism of departed individual
- Apologies or expressions of concern (implies problem)

## Playbook 3: Emergency Transition (Incapacity or Death)

### Timeline: Immediate

---

### Phase 1: Emergency Response (First 24 Hours)

**Actions:**

**1. Confirm Situation**
- Verify emergency (incapacity, death, unavailability)
- Assess severity and duration (temporary, permanent, unknown)
- Notify Board and legal counsel immediately

**2. Activate Emergency Authority**
- Activate emergency succession plan (pre-defined)
- Assign interim coverage (immediate)
- Activate authority delegation (interim has full decision-making authority)
- Notify team of emergency authority activation

**3. Secure Critical Information**
- Access emergency contact information and credentials (secure storage)
- Access critical systems and documentation
- Retrieve client lists and engagement information
- Preserve business continuity

**4. Assess Legal and HR Implications**
- Engage legal counsel (employment law, contracts, insurance)
- Assess insurance coverage (key person insurance, life insurance, disability)
- Coordinate with family (if appropriate and with sensitivity)
- Manage legal and contractual obligations

**Documentation:**
- Emergency situation confirmation
- Emergency authority activation
- Legal counsel engagement

---

### Phase 2: Stabilization & Communication (First 48-72 Hours)

**Actions:**

**1. Internal Communication**
- Announce situation to team (with sensitivity and respect)
- Introduce interim coverage (name, authority, support)
- Provide support to team (grief counseling, time off if needed)
- Maintain operational focus (work continues, clients need us)

**2. Client Communication**
- Notify key clients (with sensitivity and respect)
- Introduce interim coverage (commitment to continuity)
- Reassure operations and quality (no disruption)
- Establish communication cadence

**3. External Communication (If Appropriate)**
- Coordinate with family on public communication (if death)
- Prepare statement (if media inquiry or public interest)
- Legal counsel reviews all external communication

**4. Operational Stabilization**
- Maintain client delivery and operations
- Address immediate issues or concerns
- Monitor team morale and provide support
- Ensure business continuity

**Documentation:**
- Internal communication (announcement to team)
- Client communication (notifications)
- External communication (if applicable)

---

### Phase 3: Permanent Successor Identification (30-90 Days)

**Actions:**

**1. Assess Situation**
- Determine if emergency is temporary or permanent
- If temporary (recovery expected): Maintain interim coverage until return
- If permanent (death, permanent incapacity): Initiate permanent successor search

**2. Successor Selection**
- Review succession plan (internal candidates, external pipeline)
- Assess interim coverage (can interim become permanent?)
- Initiate search process (internal or external)
- Make selection decision and extend offer

**3. Transition to Permanent Leadership**
- Successor assumes role
- Interim coverage transitions out or returns to original role
- Stabilization and monitoring

**Timeline:**
- Internal promotion: 30-60 days
- External hire: 60-120 days

**Documentation:**
- Situation assessment
- Successor selection rationale
- Transition plan

---

### Communication Approach (Emergency Transition)

**Internal Communication:**

**Timing:**  
Announce situation within 24 hours (with sensitivity)

**Message:**

- Updates will be provided (as situation evolves)

**Tone:**
- Sensitive and respectful (acknowledge gravity of situation)
- Supportive (team wellbeing is priority)
- Calm and reassuring (operations continue)

**What NOT to Say:**
- Medical details or personal information (privacy and respect)
- Speculation about prognosis or future
- Blame or criticism

---

**Client Communication:**

**Timing:**  
Notify key clients within 24-48 hours (with sensitivity)

**Approach:**
- Executive-to-executive call (Executive Leadership to client executive)
- Follow up with written communication

**Message:**
- [Name] is unable to continue in role (reason: brief, sensitive, respectful)
- [Interim coverage name] will assume role (background, commitment)
- Operations continue without disruption
- Commitment to relationship and quality
- Regular communication and support

**Tone:**
- Sensitive and respectful (acknowledge situation)
- Confident and reassuring (operations continue)
- Appreciative (thank client for partnership and understanding)

**What NOT to Say:**
- Medical details or personal information (privacy and respect)

## Stabilization Period Expectations

### First 30 Days

**Focus:**
- Operational continuity (maintain client delivery and quality)
- Team stability (provide support and reassurance)
- Client reassurance (communicate regularly, maintain relationships)
- Knowledge transfer (interim coverage ramps up quickly)

**Success Indicators:**
- No client escalations or dissatisfaction
- No delivery disruptions or quality issues
- Team morale stable (no attrition or dysfunction)
- Interim coverage performing effectively

---

### 30-90 Days

**Focus:**
- Permanent successor identification (if not already done)
- Transition to permanent leadership (if successor identified)
- Normalization (operations return to steady state)
- Post-transition review (lessons learned)

**Success Indicators:**
- Permanent successor in place (or search in progress)
- Client satisfaction maintained or improved
- Team morale stable or improving
- Operations normalized

---

### 90+ Days

**Focus:**
- Permanent leadership established
- Continuous improvement (implement lessons learned)
- Long-term stability (no lingering transition issues)

**Success Indicators:**
- Permanent successor performing effectively
- Client relationships stable or strengthened
- Team morale positive
- Transition complete

## Governance Oversight During Transition

### Board Responsibility

**During Planned Transition:**
- Approve transition plan and timeline
- Oversee successor selection (for Executive Leadership roles)
- Monitor transition progress (regular updates)
- Approve transition agreement and terms (if applicable)

**During Unplanned or Emergency Transition:**
- Activate emergency succession plan
- Approve interim coverage assignment
- Oversee accelerated successor search
- Provide strategic guidance and support

---

### Executive Leadership Responsibility

**During Planned Transition:**
- Develop transition plan and timeline
- Manage knowledge transfer and overlap
- Communicate internally and externally
- Monitor transition effectiveness

**During Unplanned or Emergency Transition:**
- Activate interim coverage immediately
- Stabilize operations and team
- Communicate internally and externally
- Initiate permanent successor search

---

### Legal Counsel Responsibility

**During All Transitions:**
- Advise on employment law and contractual implications
- Review transition agreements and severance (if applicable)
- Review external communication (legal risk)
- Manage legal and regulatory compliance
